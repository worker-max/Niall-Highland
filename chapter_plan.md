Chapter Plan for Draft One

Chapter One establishes the host stand as the first act of hospitality, grounding the reader in Devon’s early experience and showing how greetings, wait handling, and first impressions shape the night.

Chapter Two explores timing, water, and eye contact as the emotional currency of service, emphasizing how attention and pacing create trust for guests.

Chapter Three frames service as a performance that should feel human rather than scripted, with focus on rhythm, recovery, and guiding the room’s energy.

Chapter Four reframes upselling as care-driven recommendation, showing how reading the table and offering meaningful choices lifts both guest experience and check average.

Chapter Five centers the mid-meal dead zone as a key inflection point, teaching how to maintain momentum and prevent guests from feeling invisible.

Chapter Six focuses on the final ten minutes of service, highlighting the check drop, smooth exits, and sincere gratitude as the lasting impression.

Chapter Seven addresses front-of-house burnout, showing how culture, training, and support translate directly into guest experience and staff retention.

Chapter Eight makes the case for bold, visible hospitality, emphasizing intentional moments that make guests feel seen and remembered.

Chapter Nine takes a practical, consultative look at fast fixes and operational rhythms, illustrating how small adjustments quickly improve consistency.

Chapter Ten closes by tying hospitality to legacy, reinforcing that caring for guests and staff creates a business and culture that endure.
