Companion Interview Guide for Devon

Purpose
This guide collects interview prompts and conversation starters so Devon’s lived experience can be layered into Draft 2. It is designed to pull personal stories, voice, and specific details that can be woven into the narrative inserts for each chapter. The questions are meant to be answered conversationally and in Devon’s natural voice.

Part 1: Core Story and Voice (Essence of the Book)
1) When did you first realize you were always “noticing everything” in restaurants, even as a guest?
2) What does hospitality mean to you now, compared to when you first started?
3) What’s the most generous, human, simple act of service you ever witnessed that stuck with you?
4) What’s a moment you failed a guest and learned something that still guides you today?
5) Describe the moment you knew you wanted to advocate for front-of-house workers, not just work as one.
6) When you think of “hospitality that people can feel,” what is the clearest image in your mind?
7) Which part of restaurant work do you think is most misunderstood by guests? By owners?
8) What’s a common misconception about service that drives you quietly crazy?
9) What do you want restaurant workers to feel when they finish this book?
10) What do you want guests to feel when they finish this book?

Part 2: Introduction-Level Questions (Framing Devon’s Lens)
These questions are designed to enrich the introduction with specific memories, details, and voice.
1) What is the most vivid restaurant smell that instantly brings you back in time? Where were you?
2) What is the first restaurant job moment you can still feel in your body? (nerves, excitement, dread, pride)
3) What was the “Littleton version” of hospitality that you absorbed as a teen?
4) What did Boston teach you about pace and professionalism that Littleton didn’t?
5) What did Charleston teach you about warmth and rhythm that Boston didn’t?
6) What’s the most “Devon line” you’ve ever said to a guest when trying to make them feel seen?
7) What does it feel like to sit down in a restaurant and successfully turn your radar off? Has it ever happened?
8) What are the first three things you notice when you walk in the door as a guest?
9) What makes a restaurant feel like “home” to you, even if you’re there only once?
10) If the book is a love letter and an intervention, what would you say are the three “I love yous” and the three “we need to talk” moments?

Part 3: Chapter Narrative Inserts (2-Page Story Prompts)
These prompts aim to gather personal narratives that can be inserted throughout each chapter to individualize the story.

Chapter One: The Host Stand Is Not a Mood Ring
Conversation starters
- Describe your first host stand in detail. What did it look like, sound like, smell like?
- What did you learn the first time a guest pushed back on the way you greeted them?
- Tell a story about a night when the host stand set the whole room’s tone.
Story prompts
- A time you watched a host save a night with a single sentence.
- A time a host’s tone made a guest feel small, and how it changed the evening.
- The most memorable solo diner you’ve welcomed (and what you did differently).

Chapter Two: Water, Eye Contact, and Hope
Conversation starters
- Share a moment when timing made you look like a rockstar even though the night was chaotic.
- Describe a time you felt invisible as a guest because of eye contact (or lack of it).
Story prompts
- A table you nearly lost because you delayed the first water drop.
- A guest you helped feel safe with a single refill or a look.
- A small phrase you stopped using because you realized it landed wrong.

Chapter Three: Don’t Just Serve—Host the Damn Show
Conversation starters
- Who was the best “host of the room” you ever worked with? What did they do differently?
- What’s a moment you felt a dining room “lock in” as a team?
Story prompts
- A night when a server’s calm performance held the room together.
- A time you recovered a guest experience by acknowledging a mistake with humor.
- A moment when your own confidence changed the table’s energy.

Chapter Four: Upsell Me, Baby (But Don’t Be Weird About It)
Conversation starters
- What’s the best upsell you ever received? What made it feel good?
- When did you learn upselling could be care instead of pressure?
Story prompts
- A table that wanted to celebrate and how you helped guide them there.
- A time you avoided pushing a guest and still raised the check by being thoughtful.
- A story where honesty about the menu built trust.

Chapter Five: The Mid-Meal Crisis
Conversation starters
- Describe the “dead zone” in a meal from your perspective as a server.
- When have you seen a mid-meal check-in turn a night around?
Story prompts
- A table you lost because you disappeared between courses.
- A time you kept a table energized with a simple mid-meal reset.
- A specific moment when you realized the middle of the meal needs intention.

Chapter Six: Don’t Make Me Chase the Check
Conversation starters
- What’s your most awkward check-drop memory (as server or guest)?
- What does a perfect goodbye look like to you?
Story prompts
- A time you were too fast with a check and the guest felt rushed.
- A time you were too slow with a check and it changed the tip.
- A moment when a sincere thank you made the end of the night feel complete.

Chapter Seven: Why Your Staff Is Tired (and Why Your Guests Are Too)
Conversation starters
- The earliest moment you felt front-of-house burnout and knew it wasn’t sustainable.
- A manager who protected the team’s energy, and how it changed service.
Story prompts
- A time you felt unsupported and how it showed up in your guest interactions.
- A story where training or coaching turned a struggling server into a strong one.
- A time a team felt “in sync” because the culture was healthy.

Chapter Eight: Hospitality Should Be In Your Face (In the Best Way)
Conversation starters
- What’s the most “bold hospitality” moment you’ve ever given or received?
- How do you describe the difference between warmth and intrusion?
Story prompts
- A tiny gesture you made that became a guest’s favorite part of the night.
- A place you love because it makes you feel known.
- A time you watched a guest respond to being seen and named.

Chapter Nine: If I Ran Your Restaurant for a Day
Conversation starters
- The first three things you always notice when you walk into a dining room.
- A small operational change you made that had an outsized impact.
Story prompts
- A consulting moment where a simple fix transformed a night.
- A time you stepped into a broken system and found the real bottleneck.
- A moment you saw a team go from chaotic to steady after one change.

Chapter Ten: Fixing Your Service So I Can Finally Relax
Conversation starters
- Describe your dream guest experience in full sensory detail.
- What would it mean to you personally to walk into a restaurant and fully relax?
Story prompts
- A night when you felt truly taken care of as a guest.
- A moment you saw hospitality become legacy (a place people returned to for years).
- Your most hopeful vision of what this industry can be.

Part 4: Short-Form Voice Prompts (To Capture Devon’s Sound)
These are designed to collect Devon’s phrasing and humor, Bourdain-meets-Tina-Fey-with-heart tone.
1) Finish the sentence: “The host stand is basically…”
2) Finish the sentence: “If you want to know whether a restaurant is good, watch how they…”
3) What’s a line you say in your head when you see a slow water refill?
4) What’s your funniest “I cannot believe this happened” restaurant moment?
5) What’s your favorite compliment a guest ever gave you?
6) Describe your ideal greeting in one sentence.
7) Describe your ideal goodbye in one sentence.

Part 5: Guidance for the Interview
- Aim for sensory details: the smell, the sound, the temperature of the room.
- Pull one scene per chapter that includes a specific guest interaction.
- Capture Devon’s exact phrasing when it’s sharp, warm, and human.
- Emphasize real moments over dramatic ones.
- End each answer with: what did you learn and what would you do differently now?
